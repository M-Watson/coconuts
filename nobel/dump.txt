				<div id="square_header">
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p class="ingress">The Nobel Prize in Physics has been awarded 112 times to 210 Nobel Laureates between 1901 and 2018. John Bardeen is the only Nobel Laureate who has been awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics twice, in 1956 and 1972. This means that a total of 209 individuals have received the Nobel Prize in Physics. Click on the links to get more information.</p>
</div>
<div style="clear: both">
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/physics/laureates/2018/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2018</a></h3>
<p>&#8220;for groundbreaking inventions in the field of laser physics&#8221;</p>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2018/ashkin/facts/">Arthur Ashkin</a> &#8220;for the optical tweezers and their application to biological systems&#8221;</p>
<p><a href="//www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2018/ashkin/facts/">Gérard Mourou</a> and <a href="//www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2018/strickland/facts/">Donna Strickland</a> &#8220;for their method of generating high-intensity, ultra-short optical pulses&#8221;</p>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/chemistry/laureates/2018/">The Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2018</a></h3>
<p>The 2018 Nobel Prize in Chemistry has not been awarded yet. It will be announced on Wednesday 3 October, 11:45 a.m. at the earliest.</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2017/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2017</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2017/weiss/facts/">Rainer Weiss</a>, <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2017/barish/facts/">Barry C. Barish</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2017/thorne/facts/">Kip S. Thorne</a> &#8220;for decisive contributions to the LIGO detector and the observation of gravitational waves&#8221;</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2016/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2016</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2016/thouless/facts/">David J. Thouless</a>, <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2016/haldane/facts/">F. Duncan M. Haldane</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2016/kosterlitz/facts/">J. Michael Kosterlitz </a>&#8220;for theoretical discoveries of topological phase transitions and topological phases of matter&#8221;</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2015/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2015</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2015/kajita/facts/">Takaaki Kajita</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2015/mcdonald/facts/">Arthur B. McDonald</a> &#8220;for the discovery of neutrino oscillations, which shows that neutrinos have mass&#8221;</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2014/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2014</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2014/akasaki/facts/">Isamu Akasaki</a>, <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2014/amano/facts/">Hiroshi Amano</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2014/nakamura/facts/">Shuji Nakamura</a> &#8220;for the invention of efficient blue light-emitting diodes which has enabled bright and energy-saving white light sources&#8221;</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2013/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2013</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2013/englert/facts/">François Englert</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2013/higgs/facts/">Peter W. Higgs</a> &#8220;for the theoretical discovery of a mechanism that contributes to our understanding of the origin of mass of subatomic particles, and which recently was confirmed through the discovery of the predicted fundamental particle, by the ATLAS and CMS experiments at CERN&#8217;s Large Hadron Collider&#8221;</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2012/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2012</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2012/haroche/facts/">Serge Haroche</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2012/wineland/facts/">David J. Wineland</a>&#8220;for ground-breaking experimental methods that enable measuring and manipulation of individual quantum systems&#8221;</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2011/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2011</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2011/perlmutter/facts/">Saul Perlmutter</a>, <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2011/schmidt/facts/">Brian P. Schmidt</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2011/riess/facts/">Adam G. Riess</a>&#8220;for the discovery of the accelerating expansion of the Universe through observations of distant supernovae&#8221;</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2010/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2010</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2010/geim/facts/">Andre Geim</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2010/novoselov/facts/">Konstantin Novoselov</a> &#8220;for groundbreaking experiments regarding the two-dimensional material graphene&#8221;</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2009/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2009</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2009/kao/facts/">Charles Kuen Kao</a>&#8220;for groundbreaking achievements concerning the transmission of light in fibers for optical communication&#8221;</p>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2009/boyle/facts/">Willard S. Boyle</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2009/smith/facts/">George E. Smith</a>&#8220;for the invention of an imaging semiconductor circuit &#8211; the CCD sensor&#8221;</p>
</div>
<div class="by_year">
<h3><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2008/">The Nobel Prize in Physics 2008</a></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2008/nambu/facts/">Yoichiro Nambu</a> &#8220;for the discovery of the mechanism of spontaneous broken symmetry in subatomic physics&#8221;</p>
<p><a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2008/kobayashi/facts/">Makoto Kobayashi</a> and <a href="https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/physics/2008/maskawa/facts/">Toshihide Maskawa</a> &#8220;for the discovery of the origin of the broken symmetry which predicts the existence of at least three families of quarks in nature&#8221;</p>
</div>
